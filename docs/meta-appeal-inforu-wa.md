**What 7.e.i.3 actually alleges:** that this app exists to bypass a restriction
Meta previously placed on *another* app or account of yours. So the appeal must
do two things: (1) establish the app's legitimate purpose, and (2) directly
rebut the "circumvention" link — i.e., show this app is **not** a workaround for
a prior enforced app/account.

## Notes / cautions

- **One honest, complete submission beats several rushed ones.** Repeated thin
  appeals read as "attempting to circumvent enforcement," which is the exact
  thing being alleged.
- **Do not** spin up a replacement app, change the App ID, or move the same
  workflow to another Business account to keep messaging while this is open —
  under 7.e.i.3 that is itself the violation and can escalate to the whole
  Business account being disabled.
- If the WhatsApp messaging here is **cold/unsolicited outreach** to people who
  did not opt in, that is very likely the root cause behind the original
  enforcement. No appeal wording fixes that — the underlying practice has to
  change to explicit opt-in before reinstatement is realistic.
- Reinstatement is Meta's decision; this draft maximizes the chance of a fair
  re-review but cannot guarantee an outcome.
